Chewsy

I built Chewsy in protest to the homogenization of our personal taste. Instead of the user finding his or her next meal by average ratings or reviews, they are presented with a single picture of an actual meal from restaurants within their search query.

calling for youclose

Cover art for my single Calling For You. Goal was to enhance the feeling of longing in the 21st century by blending a wifi signal and a heart.

RIFOMEclose

Cover art for another single of mine. Goal was create a retro feel that matched the song but with a modern minimalist design.

come togetherclose

Art concept for a collection of Beatles covers I produced with the artist Daphne Willis. Concept combines Daphne's signature goggles and the classic Abbey Road photo. A rainbow effect was added to stress the idea of 'coming together' during the time of LGBTQ rights.

good girl clementine!close

A social media advertisement I created for the dog bandana company Good Girl Clementine. Goal was create a lively and fun feel that engages the viewer but doesn't distract from the product.

good girl clementine!close

Web header concept designed for the dog bandana company Good Girl Clementine. Wanted to find a simple way to emphasize the company's playful nature. The dashes above the logo symbolize a ball being thrown for a dog and the bounces it takes. Logo was also design by me.

a farrell taleclose

Simple logo I created for my sister's wedding. Winter fairy-tale themed, my goal was to create something whimsical without the kitsch. The pine forest background symbolizes the cold winter while the gold whismical lettering help warm the overall tone.

About Me

Before I was a developer, I was a musician. I wasn’t a famous frontman. I didn’t headline. I was a working musician. Which means like many coders, I had to be creative under pressure, meet tight deadlines, and collaborate with all personality types at all hours. Over 11 years, I learned how to be a professional. To treat every show, whether empty or standing-room only, like Radio City. And to make every client feel like a star. In the end, I didn’t leave music. I became a coder for the same reason I started touring: I love the work.
